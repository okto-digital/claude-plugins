# Domain: Competitive Landscape

**Purpose:** Validate that the brief identifies real competitors, understands their web presence strengths and weaknesses, and defines a clear differentiation strategy. Without competitive context, the website risks blending in or missing obvious opportunities.

---

## Priority Definitions

- **CRITICAL:** Brief cannot be accurate without this. Must resolve before drafting.
- **IMPORTANT:** Brief will be thinner without this. Resolve in second pass if time allows.
- **NICE-TO-HAVE:** Useful depth. Surface once, accept skip.

---

## 1. Competitor Identification

| Checkpoint | Priority |
|---|---|
| At least 3 named competitors identified | CRITICAL |
| Competitor website URLs provided | CRITICAL |
| Mix of direct competitors (same service/market) and indirect competitors (alternative solutions) | IMPORTANT |
| Local vs. national vs. international competitors distinguished | IMPORTANT |
| Any "aspirational" competitors (companies whose web presence you admire, even outside your industry) | NICE-TO-HAVE |

## 2. Competitor Web Presence Analysis

| Checkpoint | Priority |
|---|---|
| Competitor strengths to match or exceed | CRITICAL |
| Competitor weaknesses or gaps to exploit | CRITICAL |
| Design quality and modernity of competitor sites | IMPORTANT |
| Content depth and quality on competitor sites | IMPORTANT |
| Features or functionality competitors offer (booking, chat, portals, etc.) | IMPORTANT |
| Mobile experience quality of competitor sites | NICE-TO-HAVE |

## 3. Differentiation Strategy

| Checkpoint | Priority |
|---|---|
| Clear positioning statement: why choose you over competitors? | CRITICAL |
| Unique selling points that competitors cannot easily replicate | IMPORTANT |
| Specific claims or proof points that support differentiation | IMPORTANT |
| Tone or personality differentiation (if offerings are similar) | NICE-TO-HAVE |

## 4. Market Dynamics

| Checkpoint | Priority |
|---|---|
| Is the market crowded or underserved online? | IMPORTANT |
| Are competitors investing heavily in their web presence? | NICE-TO-HAVE |
| Search landscape: who ranks for your key terms? | IMPORTANT |
| Any disruptors or new entrants changing the market? | NICE-TO-HAVE |

## 5. Competitive Content and Messaging

| Checkpoint | Priority |
|---|---|
| Common messaging patterns across competitor sites | IMPORTANT |
| Claims competitors make that you can counter | NICE-TO-HAVE |
| Content gaps: topics competitors do not cover well | IMPORTANT |
| Social proof strategies competitors use (testimonials, case studies, logos) | NICE-TO-HAVE |

---

## Question Templates

**Who are your top 3 competitors, and can you share their website URLs?**
- Option A: Direct competitors offering the same services in the same market -- since we need to benchmark against your closest rivals
- Option B: A mix of direct competitors and companies you admire for their web presence (even in different industries) -- since both inform design and content strategy

**What do your competitors do well on their websites that you want to match or exceed?**
- Option A: Strong visual design and professional imagery -- their sites look modern and trustworthy
- Option B: Deep, helpful content that answers customer questions -- they are winning on expertise and helpfulness

**What do your competitors do poorly or miss entirely on their websites?**
- Option A: Their sites are outdated, slow, or hard to navigate -- there is a clear opportunity to stand out on user experience
- Option B: They all look and sound the same -- there is an opportunity to differentiate on personality, brand voice, or unique content

**Why should a customer choose you over the competition?**
- Option A: You offer something specific that competitors do not (specialized expertise, unique process, exclusive product)
- Option B: You deliver a better experience (faster response, more personal service, better results, stronger guarantees)

**When someone searches Google for your type of service, who shows up first?**
- Option A: Competitors dominate search -- you need to compete for visibility through SEO and content
- Option B: The space is relatively open -- there is an opportunity to own key search terms early
