# Substage 3.8 — UX/UI Patterns & Benchmarks

**Code:** R8
**Slug:** UX
**Output:** `research/R8-UX.txt`

**MCP tools:** none required; web-crawler (required)

---

## Purpose

Two distinct lenses working together. The **behavioural UX** lens analyses how sites work — navigation, information architecture, conversion flows, heuristics, mobile patterns — grounded in persona research from R7-Audience. The **visual UI** lens analyses how sites look — colours, typography, imagery, spacing, brand consistency — across client, all locked competitors, and reference sites.

All locked competitors are included (vs ranks 1–3 in other substages) because visual benchmarking needs the broadest sample.

## Methodology

Dispatch `web-crawler` for each site. Analyse through two lenses:

### Behavioural UX — how sites work

Areas to explore: navigation patterns and structure, information architecture and content hierarchy, conversion flows and CTAs, mobile UX (grounded in persona device preferences from R7), usability heuristics (Nielsen's 10 as a reference lens), practical accessibility beyond R5's technical WCAG scan.

Cross-reference findings against persona journey maps from R7-Audience — where do user expectations and site reality diverge?

### Visual UI — how sites look

Areas to explore: colour palette and mood, typography hierarchy, imagery style and quality, layout density and spacing, iconography and illustration, animation and micro-interactions, brand consistency across pages.

Capture specifics where detectable (hex values, font families, grid patterns) — downstream concept work needs concrete reference points, not adjectives.

### Gap analysis

Synthesise across both lenses. What patterns do competitors share that the client lacks? What does no one do well? Where does the visual landscape leave room for differentiation?
