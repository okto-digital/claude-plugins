## Purpose

Two distinct lenses working together. The **behavioural UX** lens analyses how sites work — navigation, information architecture, conversion flows, heuristics, mobile patterns — grounded in persona research from R7-Audience. The **visual UI** lens analyses how sites look — colours, typography, imagery, spacing, brand consistency — across client, all locked competitors, and reference sites.

All locked competitors are included (vs ranks 1–3 in other substages) because visual benchmarking needs the broadest sample.

## Methodology — Behavioural UX

### Step 1: Navigation patterns

Dispatch `web-crawler` for each site. Record:
- Navigation type — mega menu, simple nav, sticky header, hamburger on desktop
- Primary navigation items and structure
- Secondary navigation and footer patterns
- Search functionality presence and placement

### Step 2: Information architecture

- Above the fold content — what is prioritised on homepage and high value pages
- Content hierarchy depth — clicks to reach key conversion points
- Page structure patterns — how content is organised and sequenced
- Cross-reference against persona journey map stages from R7-Audience

### Step 3: Conversion patterns

- CTA placement, visual style, and language
- Form length and complexity on conversion pages
- Checkout or contact flow structure where visible
- Friction points in current flows

### Step 4: Mobile UX patterns

Assess in context of persona device preferences from R7-Audience:
- Mobile navigation behaviour
- Tap target sizes and thumb zone usage
- Content prioritisation on mobile vs desktop
- Mobile-specific interaction patterns

### Step 5: Heuristics assessment

Light pass against Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics for client and all locked competitors. Flags obvious violations only:
1. Visibility of system status
2. Match between system and real world
3. User control and freedom
4. Consistency and standards
5. Error prevention
6. Recognition rather than recall
7. Flexibility and efficiency
8. Aesthetic and minimalist design
9. Help users recognise and recover from errors
10. Help and documentation

### Step 6: Accessibility UX

Beyond technical WCAG from R5-Technology — practical UX accessibility signals:
- Font sizes and readability
- Colour contrast as experienced by user
- Clear focus states and keyboard navigation signals
- Logical reading order and content flow

## Methodology — Visual UI

### Step 7: Colour palette

For each site identify:
- Primary brand colours (hex values where detectable)
- Secondary and accent colours
- Background and text colours
- Overall colour mood — warm, cool, neutral, bold, muted

### Step 8: Typography

- Primary font families (heading and body)
- Font weight and style patterns
- Typography hierarchy — heading vs body sizing and contrast
- Overall typographic mood — modern, traditional, technical, friendly

### Step 9: Imagery and photography

- Stock vs original photography
- Image quality and consistency
- Subject matter and mood
- Relevance to brand positioning

### Step 10: Layout and spacing

- Layout density — tight and information-dense vs spacious and minimal
- Grid patterns and alignment consistency
- White space usage — generous vs compressed

### Step 11: Iconography and illustration

- Icon style — line, filled, custom, generic
- Illustration presence and style
- Consistency of visual language

### Step 12: Animation and interaction

- Hover states and micro-interactions
- Scroll animations and transitions
- Perceived quality and modernity signals

### Step 13: Brand consistency

- How consistently the visual identity is applied across pages
- Inconsistencies noted as weaknesses
