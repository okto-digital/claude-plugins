# EEAT Interview Methodology

**Purpose:** Extract unique expertise, experience, and perspective from the operator/client through pointed questions. Transforms generic content into genuinely unique oktodigital content.

**EEAT:** Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness (Google's content quality framework)

---

## Core Principle

Generic questions produce generic answers. Pointed questions extract unique details that make content impossible to replicate.

---

## Question Categories

### 1. Technical Content Questions

Use when the topic involves tools, frameworks, or technical approaches.

- "This article recommends [specific tool/framework]. What does oktodigital use instead and why?"
- "Author suggests [specific approach]. Have you found cases where [different approach] works better? Give me a specific recent project example."
- "Research mentions [pain point]. What specific client situation have you seen where this played out? Walk me through it."

### 2. Process Content Questions

Use when the topic involves workflows, timelines, or methodologies.

- "Article outlines [X] steps for [process]. Walk me through oktodigital's EXACT steps -- are there more? Fewer? What's different?"
- "Research says [X] typically takes [timeframe]. What's YOUR typical timeline and what factors affect it?"
- "They recommend starting with [A]. Do you start with [A] or [B]? Tell me about a recent project where you did this."

### 3. Case Study / Example Questions

Use when you need real-world evidence and specific results.

- "This example is from [industry]. Do you have a client in [similar industry]? What were the specifics of their situation?"
- "Author's client had [problem] and achieved [result]. What's a comparable problem you solved? Walk me through the solution and actual results."
- "Research shows [statistic/outcome]. What's a real oktodigital client result that compares? Actual numbers if possible?"

### 4. Recommendation / Advice Questions

Use when the topic involves opinions, best practices, or strategic choices.

- "Article says to avoid [X]. Do you agree? Have you seen cases where [X] actually worked or failed?"
- "They recommend [specific tactic]. Does oktodigital do this? If yes, what's your twist? If no, what do you do instead?"
- "Author prioritizes [A] over [B]. What does oktodigital prioritize and why?"

### 5. Differentiator Questions

Use to surface what makes the approach genuinely different.

- "What do most agencies/companies get wrong about [topic]? What have you learned the hard way?"
- "If a client asked why they should choose you for [service], what specific example would you give?"
- "What's the most surprising outcome you've seen from [process/approach]?"

---

## Question Rules

**NEVER use vague questions:**
- "How do you do this differently?"
- "What makes you unique?"
- "What's your approach?"
- "How would oktodigital handle this?"

These produce generic, unhelpful answers.

**ALWAYS use pointed questions that:**
- Reference a specific claim, number, or recommendation from the source material
- Ask for a specific project, client, or situation
- Request actual numbers, timelines, or outcomes
- Present a binary choice ("Do you do A or B?")
- Challenge with a contrarian position ("Article says X. Do you agree?")

---

## EEAT Interview Workflow

### 1. Prepare

Read the outline or topic. Identify sections that need unique expertise:
- Claims that could be generic
- Processes that could be described by anyone
- Results that need real numbers
- Opinions that need backing

### 2. Ask (5-8 pointed questions)

Select questions from the categories above, tailored to the specific topic. Batch questions -- present 5-8 at once, not one at a time.

### 3. Incorporate

- Weave answers into content naturally
- Use direct quotes where they add authenticity
- Anonymize client details unless operator approves naming
- Replace generic claims with specific examples from answers

### 4. Flag gaps

If answers are thin on a section, flag it:

```
[EEAT GAP: This section would benefit from a specific example of [topic].
Current content uses general claims. Consider adding a case study or metric.]
```

---

## Quality Standard

Content passes EEAT when:
- At least 3 sections contain specific, non-replicable details from the interview
- No section relies entirely on generic claims
- Real project examples or client outcomes are referenced (anonymized)
- The content could not have been written by someone who has never done the work
