# okto-digital Brand Voice Definition

**Company:** okto-digital
**Industry:** Digital product studio (bespoke websites, web apps, mobile apps)
**Audience:** Small to medium businesses (B2B)

---

## Voice Personality

**okto-digital is The Approachable Expert** -- a digital studio that combines mastery with genuine warmth, curiosity with reliability. We speak like a capable colleague who really cares, not a corporate vendor or distant authority.

**Core Attributes:**
- **Reliable (40%)** -- The dependable partner who delivers on commitments
- **Genuine (35%)** -- Authentic care and honest communication, friend-to-friend
- **Curious (25%)** -- Discovery-led, question-asking, always learning

**Brand Archetype Mix:**
- Creator (50%) -- Mastery, innovation, building quality solutions
- Everyman (30%) -- Approachability, human connection, down-to-earth
- Sage (20%) -- Guidance, precision, teaching while doing

**In one sentence:** We're the capable, caring colleague who's realistic but optimistic, patient but present -- we teach as we build, and we build as partners.

**Values:**
1. **Clarity** -- Clear communication, simple solutions

## Grammar & Language Rules

**Formality: CASUAL** (clarity-focused casual with complete sentences)

**Grammar:**
- Contractions REQUIRED everywhere (we'll, you'll, it's, let's, don't)
- Can start sentences with conjunctions (And, But, So)
- Active voice strongly preferred (90%+)
- Complete sentences preferred (clarity priority)
- Fragments acceptable occasionally for impact

**Complexity:** 9th-10th grade Flesch-Kincaid (allow 10th-12th for technical blog posts)

**Jargon:** Minimal -- define when used

**Sentence structure:**
- Conversational complete sentences with conjunctions
- Varied sentence length (mix 5-8 words with 10-15 words)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Oxford comma for clarity

**Buzzwords to NEVER use:**
empower, leverage, synergy, transform, innovate, disrupt, optimize, utilize, paradigm, strategic imperatives, value-add, thought leadership, best-in-class, world-class, cutting-edge, next-generation, revolutionary, game-changing

**Preferred words:**
create, build, solve, improve, use, change, grow, help, seamless, tailored, custom, specific, clear, simple, effective

## Perspective & Relationship

**Point of View:** Balanced we/you
- 40% "you" (benefits, outcomes, customer focus)
- 30% "we" (brand identity, mission, capabilities)
- 30% neutral/implied (expert guidance, general truths)

**Power Dynamic:** Peer/Friend -- "Equals on shared journey"

**Relationship Distance:** Warm colleague -- supportive without overly familiar

**Inclusivity:** Quietly Selective -- welcoming messaging, but values naturally attract aligned clients

**Inclusive Language:** Quietly Inclusive (inclusive by default, never announced)
- Use gender-neutral language naturally (they, folks, everyone, team)
- Don't announce inclusivity commitment
- No corporate virtue signaling

## Emotional Quality

- **Warmth:** Warm (friendly colleague)
- **Energy:** Moderate (balanced, steady, capable)
- **Confidence:** Balanced (capable but collaborative)
- **Optimism:** Balanced (positive but realistic)
- **Humor:** Light on website, warm and gentle (dark/sarcastic reserved for social media and team bios only)
- **Urgency:** Patient (relaxed, thoughtful, no pressure)

**Overall:** The capable, caring colleague who's realistic but optimistic, patient but present.

---

## Pacing & Rhythm

**Information Density:** Moderate -- multiple related points, still digestible
**Tempo:** Moderate -- steady, conversational pace
**Visual Rhythm:** Moderately varied -- mix of sentence lengths, consistent paragraph size, occasional lists

Content should be efficient but never overwhelming. Respect the busy SMB audience while being thorough.

## Storytelling

**Primary:** Hero's Journey -- client is the hero, okto-digital is the guide
**Secondary:** Educational / How-To -- teach while you sell

**Positioning:**
- Client is the hero of their digital transformation story
- okto-digital is the experienced guide who helps the hero succeed
- Educate to build trust

## Rhetorical Devices

**Usage:** Moderate -- occasional well-placed metaphors and concrete analogies
**Purpose:** Explain technical concepts, make complexity accessible

**Keep analogies:**
- Relatable, simple, grounded in everyday experience
- Concrete comparisons (not abstract poetry)
- Clarity-focused, not decorative

---

## Content Principles

**Content Focus:** Balanced Mix
- Talk about what you offer (capabilities)
- Focus on customer outcomes (benefits)
- Teach how things work (process/insights)

**Transparency:** Selectively Transparent
- Open about process and learnings
- Selective about specifics (protect client confidentiality)
- Honest about challenges without oversharing

**Proof Style:** Hybrid (Process + Story, data when available)
- Show HOW you work (process explanations)
- Share client stories and narratives
- Include data when you have it (not required)

**Tone of Claims:** Collaborative with Confidence (C+)
- Confident in capability: "We'll build..."
- Collaborative in approach: "...let's figure out together"

**Content Attitude:** Educational on website, opinionated on social
- Website/client-facing: Educational, pragmatic, helpful
- Social media: Hot takes, opinions, challenge norms

---

## B2B Positioning

**Approach:** Humanized B2B with Personality (B+)

Personality-forward B2B that stands out while staying credible. Lean toward casual/human but maintain business appropriateness.

**DO:**
- Lead with personality and warmth
- Heavy you/we usage, minimal corporate speak
- Show real humans behind the business
- Use analogies and direct language

**DON'T:**
- Start cold emails with "Hey!" (too casual for first contact)
- Use overly casual language in proposals
- Abandon professionalism entirely

**Strategic advantage:** Most B2B competitors use safe, forgettable "professional warmth." You stand out with personality while maintaining credibility.

## Voice Examples

### What we sound like

- "We'll build a solution that works for you. Let's start with discovery to figure out exactly what you need."
- "We get it -- outdated software is frustrating. Let's build something that actually works for you."
- "We build custom apps tailored to your business. Each project starts with discovery -- we ask questions, listen carefully, and map out exactly what you need."
- "We'll build a solution that works. Let's figure out exactly what you need together."
- "Their checkout had 7 steps and 60% abandonment. We rebuilt it with 3 steps. Abandonment dropped to 23%. Here's exactly what we changed and why."

### What we DON'T sound like

- "We will be pleased to assist you in determining optimal requirements." (too formal)
- "We'll leverage innovative synergies to transform and empower your organization." (buzzwords)
- "Our methodology ensures you follow best practices." (authority figure)
- "We helped a client improve their website and they saw great results." (vague)
- "We transformed their business with our innovative solution." (okto-digital as hero)
- "Limited time offer! Contact us today before it's too late!" (pressure tactics)
- "We guarantee optimal results through our proven methodology." (too authoritative)
- "We might be able to help if you think we're a good fit." (too modest)
- "Apps are programs on your phone. We make them go beep boop." (patronizing)

### Channel-specific examples

**Website & service pages:**
"Let's build something that actually works for your business. We'll start with discovery -- think of it like mapping the route before the road trip."

**Blog & case studies:**
"They were manually processing 200 orders per day in spreadsheets. We built a custom system that automated 80% of it. Here's the 3-month build process and what we'd do differently next time."

**Team bios:**
"Stefan has spent 7 years explaining why 'make the logo bigger' isn't actually design feedback. He's gotten very good at smiling through it."
