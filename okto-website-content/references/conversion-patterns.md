## CTA Wording

### Action Verbs That Convert

**High-performing:** Get, Start, Download, Schedule, Claim
**Low-performing:** Submit, Click Here, Learn More

### Specificity Increases Clicks

Generic CTAs underperform specific ones by 100-170%:

```
Generic: "Contact Us"
Specific: "Schedule Your Discovery Call"

Generic: "Get Started"
Specific: "Start Your Free 30-Day Trial"
```

**Why specificity works:** Reduces uncertainty, sets expectations, implies value, feels less risky.

## Brand Voice-Compliant CTAs

### Strong CTAs (Decision Stage)

```
"Schedule Your Discovery Call" -- action verb + specific outcome
"Get Your Custom Quote" -- direct + personalized
"Start Your Project Today" -- immediate + ownership
"Book Free Consultation" -- no-risk + valuable
```

### Soft CTAs (Awareness/Consideration)

```
"See How It Works" -- curious, low-pressure
"Explore Our Process" -- genuine, transparent
"Read Customer Stories" -- social proof, educational
"Check Out Examples" -- casual, exploratory
```

### Voice Violations (Avoid)

```
"Leverage Our Revolutionary Platform" -- buzzwords
"Unlock Amazing Results Now!" -- hype + pressure
"Synergize Your Workflow" -- jargon
```

## CTA Placement

**Service pages:** Primary CTA above fold, secondary after benefits section (50-60% down), reinforcement at end.

**Blog posts (2,000+ words):** 1 CTA after introduction, 1 every 600-800 words, 1 at end. Don't exceed 1 per 400 words.

**Landing pages:** Primary CTA above fold (hero), repeat every 500-600 words (same CTA, different context), final at bottom.

**Single CTA focus:** Pages with 1 CTA convert 2.6x higher than pages with 2-3 CTAs. Keep secondary CTAs minimal.

## Trust Signals

### Specific Beats Generic

```
Generic: "Great service, highly recommend!" -- weak
Specific: "Increased our email open rates by 18% in 3 months" -- 4x more effective
```

### Placement

- **Near primary CTA:** Strongest conversion impact
- **After benefits section:** Second strongest
- **Distributed throughout:** Moderate impact
- **Client logos near CTA:** +18% higher conversion than logos alone

### What Works

```
Strong: "50,000+ customers trust us"
Strong: "4.8/5 average rating from 1,245 reviews"
Weak: "Thousands of happy customers"
Weak: "Highly rated"
```

## Page Structure by Goal

### Landing Page (Conversion-Focused)

- Above fold (60%): Value proposition, key benefit, social proof, primary CTA
- Mid-content (30%): 3-5 benefit bullets, visual, secondary social proof, repeat CTA
- End (10%): Final CTA, urgency if applicable, trust signals
- **Total: 500-1,200 words**

### Pillar Page (SEO-Focused)

- Above fold (15%): Hook, TLDR, soft CTA
- Mid-content (75%): Comprehensive coverage, keyword-rich headers, internal links, secondary CTA every 800 words
- End (10%): Summary, related content, moderate CTA
- **Total: 2,000-5,000 words**

### Service/Product Page (Balanced)

- Above fold (30%): Value proposition, key benefits, primary CTA, trust signal
- Mid-content (50%): Detailed features, how it works, use cases, FAQ, secondary CTA
- End (20%): Summary, final CTA, testimonials, trust signals
- **Total: 1,000-2,000 words**

## Readability Target

**oktodigital target:** 9th-10th grade Flesch-Kincaid (see voice-definition.md)

**Quick rules for hitting target:**
- Active voice over passive ("Our team designed..." not "The website was designed by...")
- Simple words over complex ("use" not "utilize", "help" not "facilitate")
- Short sentences (mix 5-8 words with 10-15 words)
- Split long sentences into two
