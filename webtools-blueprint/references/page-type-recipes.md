# Page Type Recipes

Proven section sequences for common page types. Each recipe provides a recommended section flow, purpose annotations, typical word counts, and conversion strategy notes. Adapt based on the specific project brief and target audience.

---

## Homepage

**Purpose:** First impression, brand positioning, and visitor routing to key conversion paths.
**Typical word count:** 800-1500 words
**Conversion strategy:** Multiple soft CTAs throughout; primary CTA above fold; secondary CTAs after proof sections.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Brand promise + primary CTA. Communicate what you do, who you serve, and why it matters in under 10 seconds.
2. **Social proof bar** -- Client logos, trust badges, or key stats. Builds immediate credibility.
3. **Problem/Pain** -- Articulate the audience's challenge. Creates emotional connection.
4. **Solution/Value proposition** -- How you solve it. Bridge from problem to your offering.
5. **Services/Features overview** -- Card grid or list of key offerings. Routes visitors to detail pages.
6. **How it works/Process** -- 3-5 step process. Reduces perceived complexity.
7. **Testimonials** -- Client quotes with names, roles, companies. Social proof at decision point.
8. **Case study highlight** -- One featured result with metrics. Concrete evidence.
9. **CTA banner** -- Strong conversion prompt before footer. Catches scrollers.

**Common variations:**
- Add Stats/Numbers section after Hero for data-driven audiences
- Replace Case study with Portfolio grid for visual industries
- Add Blog preview for content-heavy brands
- Add FAQ before final CTA for high-consideration purchases

## Service Page

**Purpose:** Convert visitors interested in a specific service. Demonstrate expertise and reduce risk.
**Typical word count:** 1000-2000 words
**Conversion strategy:** Service-specific CTA repeated 2-3 times; address objections before asking for commitment.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Service name + one-sentence value proposition + service-specific CTA.
2. **Problem/Pain** -- The specific problem this service solves. Audience should see themselves.
3. **Solution detail** -- How this service works, what it includes. Be specific.
4. **Features/Benefits** -- Key features with benefit-focused copy. Cards or icon grid.
5. **Process** -- How engagement works from first contact to delivery. Reduces uncertainty.
6. **Case study / Results** -- Specific results from this service. Metrics when possible.
7. **Testimonials** -- Service-specific client quotes.
8. **FAQ** -- Address common questions and objections for this service.
9. **CTA** -- Service-specific call to action.

**Common variations:**
- Add Pricing section if pricing is transparent
- Add Comparison table if differentiating from alternatives
- Add Team section if expertise/credentials matter (consulting, professional services)
- Split into sub-service pages if the service has distinct tiers or types

## About Page

**Purpose:** Build trust, communicate values, and humanize the brand.
**Typical word count:** 800-1500 words
**Conversion strategy:** Soft CTA; this page builds trust rather than directly converting. End with a values-aligned CTA.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Company story hook. Not "About Us" as a heading -- lead with the mission or a compelling statement.
2. **Story/Mission** -- Company origin, mission, vision. Why you exist beyond making money.
3. **Values** -- 3-5 core values with explanations. Show what guides decisions.
4. **Team** -- Key team members with photos, roles, brief bios. Humanizes the brand.
5. **Stats/Numbers** -- Years in business, clients served, projects completed. Credibility.
6. **Timeline/History** -- Key milestones. Shows growth and stability.
7. **Testimonials** -- Client quotes about working with the team (relationship-focused, not just results).
8. **CTA** -- Values-aligned invitation ("Let's work together", "Join us", etc.).

**Common variations:**
- Lead with Team for people-driven businesses (consulting, agencies)
- Add Partners/Certifications for industries where credentials matter
- Skip Timeline for newer businesses; emphasize vision instead
- Add Culture section for recruitment-focused about pages

## Portfolio / Case Studies Page

**Purpose:** Showcase work and results. Provide proof of capability.
**Typical word count:** 300-500 for listing; 800-1500 per case study
**Conversion strategy:** Let results speak; CTA at end of each case study and on listing page.

**Recommended section sequence (listing page):**

1. **Hero** -- Brief intro to portfolio. "Our work speaks for itself" approach.
2. **Portfolio grid** -- Filterable grid of projects. Each card: image, title, client, result highlight.
3. **CTA** -- Invitation to discuss a similar project.

**Recommended section sequence (individual case study):**

1. **Hero** -- Project name, client, key result metric.
2. **Challenge** -- What the client needed. Set the context.
3. **Solution** -- What you did. Be specific about approach.
4. **Process** -- How you executed. Shows methodology.
5. **Results** -- Measurable outcomes. Metrics, screenshots, testimonials.
6. **Client testimonial** -- Quote from the client about the experience.
7. **CTA** -- "Want similar results?"

## Landing Page

**Purpose:** Single conversion goal. Everything serves one action.
**Typical word count:** 500-1200 words
**Conversion strategy:** Aggressive. Every section drives toward the one CTA. Remove all navigation distractions.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Clear offer + primary CTA. No ambiguity about what visitor gets.
2. **Problem/Pain** -- Why the visitor needs this. Emotional connection.
3. **Solution** -- What you are offering and why it is the answer.
4. **Benefits** -- 3-5 key benefits. Outcome-focused, not feature-focused.
5. **Social proof** -- Testimonials, stats, logos. Reduce risk at the decision point.
6. **How it works** -- Simple 3-step process. Make it feel easy.
7. **FAQ** -- Address objections. Remove friction.
8. **CTA** -- Final strong call to action. Urgency if appropriate.

**Common variations:**
- Add Pricing for product/offer landing pages
- Add Video for complex offers that benefit from demonstration
- Add Comparison for competitive differentiation pages
- Remove FAQ for simple, low-friction offers

## Contact Page

**Purpose:** Make it easy to get in touch. Remove all friction.
**Typical word count:** 200-400 words
**Conversion strategy:** Minimize text, maximize accessibility. Multiple contact methods.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Inviting heading + brief encouraging message. Not just "Contact Us".
2. **Contact form** -- Clean, focused form. Only ask for necessary fields.
3. **Contact details** -- Phone, email, address, map. Multiple channels.
4. **FAQ** -- Common pre-contact questions. Reduce unnecessary inquiries.

**Common variations:**
- Add Team contacts for companies with departmental routing
- Add Office locations with maps for multi-location businesses
- Add Calendar embed for appointment-based businesses
- Split into separate forms per service or inquiry type

## Blog Listing Page

**Purpose:** Content hub. Showcase expertise and drive organic traffic.
**Typical word count:** 100-200 words (intro only; posts are separate)
**Conversion strategy:** Email signup/newsletter CTA. Keep visitors in the content ecosystem.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Brief intro to the blog/resources section. What readers will find.
2. **Featured post** -- Highlighted latest or top post.
3. **Post grid** -- Filterable/searchable grid of posts. Categories, tags, or topics.
4. **Newsletter CTA** -- Email signup to stay updated.

## Product Page

**Purpose:** Convert product interest into purchase. Provide all information needed to buy.
**Typical word count:** 500-1500 words
**Conversion strategy:** Add to cart / Buy now as primary CTA. Trust elements at decision points.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Product hero** -- Product images/gallery + name + price + primary CTA (buy/add to cart).
2. **Product description** -- What it is, who it is for.
3. **Features/Specs** -- Detailed specifications or features. Tabs or accordion.
4. **Social proof** -- Reviews, ratings, user photos.
5. **Related products** -- Cross-sell and upsell opportunities.
6. **FAQ** -- Product-specific questions (shipping, returns, sizing).

---

## Pricing Page

**Purpose:** Present pricing clearly and drive plan selection.
**Typical word count:** 400-800 words
**Conversion strategy:** Highlight recommended plan. Reduce choice paralysis. Address value objections.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Clear heading. "Simple, transparent pricing" approach.
2. **Pricing table** -- Plans side by side. Highlight recommended plan. Feature comparison.
3. **FAQ** -- Pricing-specific questions (billing, cancellation, what is included).
4. **Testimonials** -- Value-focused quotes ("worth every penny", ROI stories).
5. **CTA** -- Final prompt for undecided visitors. Often a "talk to sales" option.

## Case Study (Individual)

**Purpose:** Deep-dive proof of results for a specific client or project.
**Typical word count:** 800-1500 words
**Conversion strategy:** Results-driven. CTA after seeing proof.

**Recommended section sequence:**

1. **Hero** -- Client name, project type, key headline metric.
2. **Client overview** -- Brief context on who the client is.
3. **Challenge** -- What problem they faced. Make it relatable.
4. **Approach** -- What you did and why. Show strategic thinking.
5. **Solution details** -- Implementation specifics. Show depth.
6. **Results** -- Measurable outcomes. Before/after. Metrics.
7. **Client quote** -- Testimonial from the client.
8. **CTA** -- "Want similar results? Let's talk."
