# Section Pattern Library

Catalog of individual section types used in page blueprints. Each entry covers purpose, when to use it, content requirements, visual hints, and common mistakes.

---

## Hero

**Purpose:** First impression. Communicate the core value proposition and primary call to action within seconds.

**When to use:** Every page has some form of hero. It is the first section visitors see.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline (5-12 words): Clear, benefit-driven statement
- Subheadline (15-30 words): Supporting detail or audience qualifier
- Primary CTA: One clear action
- Optional: Secondary CTA (lower commitment alternative)
- Word count: 30-80 words

**Visual hints:** Full-width, prominent heading, background image or illustration, clear CTA button.

**Common mistakes:**
- Headline too vague ("Welcome to our website")
- Too many CTAs competing for attention
- Missing subheadline that explains what you actually do
- CTA text too generic ("Learn More" instead of "Get Your Free Quote")

---

## Problem / Pain

**Purpose:** Create emotional connection by articulating the audience's challenge. Makes visitors feel understood.

**When to use:** Early in the page (positions 2-3). Before presenting the solution. Most effective on service pages, landing pages, and homepages.

**Content requirements:**
- Problem statement: What is the audience struggling with?
- Impact: What happens if this problem is not solved?
- Emotional hook: How does this problem make them feel?
- Word count: 80-150 words

**Visual hints:** Text-focused, possibly with a supporting illustration. Clean layout that lets the message land.

**Common mistakes:**
- Being too dramatic or fear-based
- Describing a problem the audience does not actually have
- Spending too long on the problem without transitioning to the solution
- Using jargon the audience does not understand

---

## Solution / Value Proposition

**Purpose:** Bridge from problem to offering. Show how you solve the specific problem just described.

**When to use:** Immediately after Problem/Pain section. Positions 3-4.

**Content requirements:**
- Clear statement of what you offer
- How it addresses the specific problem
- Key differentiator (why you, not alternatives)
- Word count: 80-150 words

**Visual hints:** Image + text layout. May include a product screenshot, diagram, or illustration.

**Common mistakes:**
- Jumping to features without connecting back to the problem
- Being too abstract ("We deliver solutions")
- Not differentiating from competitors

---

## Features / Services

**Purpose:** Detail specific capabilities or service offerings. Helps visitors understand what they get.

**When to use:** After establishing the value proposition. Positions 4-6.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-6 features or services
- Per item: name, brief description (20-40 words), benefit statement
- Optional: icon or image per item
- Word count: 150-300 words total

**Visual hints:** Card grid (2x3 or 3x2), icon + text list, or alternating image + text rows.

**Common mistakes:**
- Feature-focused instead of benefit-focused descriptions
- Too many items (more than 6 creates choice overload)
- All items same length and emphasis (no prioritization)

---

## How It Works / Process

**Purpose:** Reduce perceived complexity. Show the clear path from start to result.

**When to use:** After features/services. Especially important for complex offerings or when the audience is unfamiliar with the process. Positions 5-7.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-5 numbered steps
- Per step: name (2-5 words) and brief description (15-30 words)
- Clear start and end points
- Word count: 80-150 words

**Visual hints:** Numbered steps, timeline, horizontal flow diagram, or vertical step list.

**Common mistakes:**
- Too many steps (more than 5 feels overwhelming)
- Steps too vague ("Step 1: We talk")
- Missing the outcome at the end

---

## Testimonials / Social Proof

**Purpose:** Third-party validation. Reduce perceived risk through others' experiences.

**When to use:** After presenting the offering, before asking for commitment. Positions 6-8.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-4 testimonials
- Per testimonial: quote (20-50 words), name, role/company, optional photo
- Quotes should be specific (mention results, experience) not generic ("Great company!")
- Word count: 80-200 words total

**Visual hints:** Quote cards, carousel, or stacked quotes. Client photos increase trust.

**Common mistakes:**
- Generic quotes that could apply to any business
- No attribution (anonymous quotes have less impact)
- Too many testimonials (3 strong ones beat 10 weak ones)
- Quotes that are too long to read

---

## Case Study Highlight

**Purpose:** Concrete proof of results through a specific client story.

**When to use:** After testimonials or as an alternative to them. Positions 7-9.

**Content requirements:**
- Client name and context (1 sentence)
- Challenge (1-2 sentences)
- Key result metric (one headline number)
- Brief description of what you did
- Link to full case study (if it exists)
- Word count: 80-150 words

**Visual hints:** Card or split layout with a headline metric, brief text, and a "Read more" link.

**Common mistakes:**
- No specific metrics (just "improved results")
- Too much detail for a highlight section (save depth for the full case study page)

---

## Stats / Numbers

**Purpose:** Quick credibility through impressive metrics. Supports claims with data.

**When to use:** After hero (for immediate credibility) or before CTA (for reinforcement). Positions 2-3 or 7-8.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-4 key statistics
- Per stat: number, unit/label, optional brief context
- Numbers must be real and verifiable
- Word count: 20-50 words

**Visual hints:** Large numbers in a horizontal row. Counter animation optional.

**Common mistakes:**
- Too many stats (more than 4 dilutes impact)
- Vague stats ("Many clients" instead of "200+ clients")
- Stats that do not relate to the audience's concerns

---

## FAQ

**Purpose:** Address common questions and objections. Remove friction before the CTA.

**When to use:** Late in the page, before the final CTA. Positions 7-9.

**Content requirements:**
- 5-8 questions and answers
- Questions phrased as the audience would ask them
- Answers concise but complete (20-60 words each)
- Address real objections, not softballs
- Word count: 200-400 words total

**Visual hints:** Accordion (expand/collapse) or simple Q&A list.

**Common mistakes:**
- Questions that nobody actually asks
- Answers that are too long or too vague
- Not addressing real objections (pricing, timeline, commitment)
- Using FAQ as a dump for miscellaneous content

---

## CTA (Primary)

**Purpose:** Drive the primary conversion action. This is what the page exists for.

**When to use:** End of page. Also placed above the fold in the hero. Positions 9-10.

**Content requirements:**
- Headline: Action-oriented (4-10 words)
- Supporting text: Why act now, what happens next (15-30 words)
- Button text: Specific action ("Get Your Free Quote", not "Submit")
- Word count: 30-60 words

**Visual hints:** Full-width background, contrasting color, centered text, prominent button.

**Common mistakes:**
- Vague button text ("Submit", "Learn More")
- No supporting context (why should I click?)
- CTA that does not match the page's promise
- Too many options at the final decision point

---

## CTA (Secondary / Banner)

**Purpose:** Mid-page conversion opportunity. Catches visitors who are ready to act before reaching the bottom.

**When to use:** Between major content sections. Positions 4-6. Lighter than primary CTA.

**Content requirements:**
- Brief headline (4-8 words)
- Optional: one line of supporting text
- Button or link
- Word count: 15-30 words

**Visual hints:** Colored background band, inline with content flow, not full-page.

**Common mistakes:**
- Making it as prominent as the primary CTA (creates confusion)
- Too frequent (more than one mid-page CTA feels pushy)

## Portfolio Grid

**Purpose:** Visual showcase of work. Lets visitors browse projects.

**When to use:** Portfolio/case studies pages. Can also appear on homepage as a preview.

**Content requirements:**
- 4-12 portfolio items
- Per item: image, project title, client name, category/tag
- Optional: brief result metric
- Filterable by category (if 6+ items)

**Visual hints:** Responsive grid (2-4 columns), hover effects showing project details, click to case study.

**Common mistakes:**
- No filtering on large portfolios
- Images of inconsistent quality or size
- No context (just images without titles or descriptions)

---

## Pricing Table

**Purpose:** Present pricing options clearly. Drive plan selection.

**When to use:** Pricing pages. Can also appear on service or product pages.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-4 pricing tiers
- Per tier: name, price, feature list, CTA
- Highlight recommended tier
- Optional: comparison toggle (monthly/annual)
- Word count: 100-200 words

**Visual hints:** Side-by-side columns, recommended tier visually distinct, checkmark feature lists.

**Common mistakes:**
- Too many tiers (more than 4 creates choice paralysis)
- No recommended option highlighted
- Feature lists that are hard to compare across tiers
- Hidden fees or unclear what is included

---

## Contact Form

**Purpose:** Capture visitor information for follow-up. Make it easy to reach out.

**When to use:** Contact pages. Can also appear as a CTA section on other pages.

**Content requirements:**
- Form fields: only what is necessary (name, email, message minimum)
- Brief intro text (why fill this out, what happens next)
- Privacy note
- Submit button with specific text ("Send Message", not "Submit")
- Word count: 30-60 words (intro + labels)

**Visual hints:** Clean form layout, adequate whitespace, clear labels, visible submit button.

**Common mistakes:**
- Too many fields (every additional field reduces completion rate)
- No indication of what happens after submitting
- Generic submit button text

---

## Blog Preview

**Purpose:** Showcase recent or featured content. Drive visitors into the content ecosystem.

**When to use:** Homepage (to show thought leadership) or related content sections on other pages.

**Content requirements:**
- 3-4 recent or featured posts
- Per post: title, excerpt (20-40 words), date, category, thumbnail
- Link to full blog listing
- Word count: 80-160 words

**Visual hints:** Card grid (3 columns) or featured + list layout.

**Common mistakes:**
- Showing outdated posts (if blog is not updated regularly, do not feature dates)
- Excerpts that do not compel clicks
- No clear path to full blog listing

---

## Partners / Client Logos

**Purpose:** Quick credibility through brand association.

**When to use:** Near top of page (positions 2-3) for immediate trust, or near bottom before CTA.

**Content requirements:**
- 4-8 logos
- All logos should be recognizable to the target audience
- Optional: brief text ("Trusted by" or "Our partners")
- Word count: 5-15 words

**Visual hints:** Horizontal logo bar, grayscale logos, auto-scrolling carousel for many logos.

**Common mistakes:**
- Logos that the audience does not recognize
- Too many logos (dilutes impact)
- Inconsistent logo sizes or quality

---

## Comparison Table

**Purpose:** Differentiate from competitors or alternatives. Show why you are the better choice.

**When to use:** When the audience is comparison-shopping. Service pages, landing pages.

**Content requirements:**
- 2-4 options compared (you vs competitors, or your plans)
- 5-10 comparison criteria
- Clear visual winner (your offering)
- Word count: 50-100 words

**Visual hints:** Table with checkmarks/x-marks, your column highlighted.

**Common mistakes:**
- Unfair comparison (straw man competitor)
- Too many criteria (focus on what matters to the audience)
- No clear winner emerging

---

## Timeline / History

**Purpose:** Show company growth, project progression, or process milestones.

**When to use:** About pages (company history), case study pages (project timeline).

**Content requirements:**
- 4-8 milestones
- Per milestone: year/date, event title, brief description (10-20 words)
- Chronological order
- Word count: 60-160 words

**Visual hints:** Vertical timeline with alternating left/right content, or horizontal scrolling timeline.

**Common mistakes:**
- Too many milestones (loses impact)
- Milestones that are not meaningful to the audience
- No context for why milestones matter

---

## Team

**Purpose:** Humanize the brand. Build personal connection and trust.

**When to use:** About pages, sometimes service pages for expertise-driven businesses.

**Content requirements:**
- Key team members (3-8 people)
- Per person: photo, name, role, brief bio (20-40 words)
- Optional: social links, fun fact
- Word count: 100-300 words total

**Visual hints:** Card grid with photos, hover for details, or list with photos and bios side by side.

**Common mistakes:**
- Stock photos instead of real team photos
- Bios that are all the same length and tone
- Including too many people (focus on key faces)
- Missing photos (text-only bios feel impersonal)
